Sedation Dentistry

Is Sedation Dentistry Safe for Kids?

Your Child's Safety and Comfort Are Our Top Priority

It's natural for kids to feel a little nervous coming to the dentist. They may associate it with going to the doctor's office when they were sick or needed a shot. That's why we offer safe and effective sedation options to help anxious children feel comfortable during their dental procedures.

Sedation Options We Offer:

  • Nitrous Oxide (Laughing Gas) - Mild sedation that helps children relax while remaining conscious and responsive
  • Oral Sedation - Medication taken by mouth to reduce anxiety
  • IV Sedation - Deeper sedation for more complex procedures or highly anxious patients

Safety First

Our pediatric dentists have comprehensive education and training in sedation and anesthesia techniques. We carefully evaluate each child to determine the most appropriate sedation option and monitor them closely throughout the procedure.

Sedation dentistry allows us to:

  • Complete necessary dental work in fewer appointments
  • Reduce anxiety and fear associated with dental visits
  • Help children who have difficulty sitting still
  • Treat children with special needs more effectively

Dental Sealants

Protecting Your Child's Teeth

What Are Dental Sealants?

Dental sealants are thin, protective coatings applied to the chewing surfaces of back teeth (molars). They act as a barrier, protecting teeth from decay-causing bacteria and food particles.

Benefits of Dental Sealants:

  • Prevent cavities in molars and premolars
  • Quick and painless application
  • Can last several years with proper care
  • Especially beneficial for children and teens
  • Cost-effective preventive treatment

The Sealant Process

Applying sealants is simple, painless, and takes only a few minutes per tooth:

  1. The tooth is cleaned and dried
  2. An acidic gel is applied to roughen the tooth surface
  3. The tooth is rinsed and dried again
  4. The sealant is painted onto the tooth enamel
  5. A special light hardens the sealant

Sealants are most commonly recommended for children when their permanent molars come in, typically between ages 6 and 12.

Fluoride Treatment

Strengthening Your Child's Teeth

What Is Fluoride Treatment?

Fluoride is a natural mineral that helps prevent cavities by making tooth enamel more resistant to acid attacks from bacteria and sugars in the mouth. Professional fluoride treatments provide a higher concentration of fluoride than what's in toothpaste or drinking water.

Benefits of Fluoride Treatment:

  • Strengthens tooth enamel
  • Prevents tooth decay
  • Can reverse early signs of cavities
  • Safe and effective for children
  • Quick application during regular dental visits

What to Expect

Fluoride treatment is quick and painless. We apply a fluoride varnish, gel, or foam to your child's teeth. The entire process takes just a few minutes, and children are usually asked to avoid eating or drinking for 30 minutes afterward to allow the fluoride to fully absorb.

We typically recommend fluoride treatments every six months during regular dental check-ups, though some children at higher risk for cavities may benefit from more frequent applications.

Emergency Dental Care

We're Here When You Need Us

Dental Emergencies Happen

When your child experiences a dental emergency, prompt attention is crucial. Whether it's a knocked-out tooth, severe toothache, or injury to the mouth, our team is ready to provide immediate care.

Common Dental Emergencies:

  • Knocked-Out Tooth - Call us immediately and try to keep the tooth moist
  • Chipped or Broken Tooth - Save any pieces and rinse the mouth with warm water
  • Severe Toothache - Rinse with warm water and apply a cold compress
  • Object Stuck in Teeth - Try to gently remove with floss, never use sharp objects
  • Bitten Lip or Tongue - Clean the area gently and apply cold compress

What to Do in a Dental Emergency

  1. Stay Calm - Your child will take cues from you
  2. Call Our Office - We'll provide immediate guidance: 845-634-8900
  3. Manage Pain - Apply cold compress and give age-appropriate pain reliever if needed
  4. Come In Quickly - Time is often critical in dental emergencies

If anything arises after you leave our office, you can call us anytime. We're committed to being there for you and your family when emergencies happen.
